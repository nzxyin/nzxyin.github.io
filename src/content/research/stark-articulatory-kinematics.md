title: "STArK: Towards Synthesizing Articulatory Kinematics from Text"
authors: "Xavier Yin, Carlos Busso"
venue: "Interspeech 2026 (Oral), Sydney, Australia"
date: 2026-09-27
abstract: "Recent deep learning approaches in modeling articulatory kinematics have shown success in mapping speech audio to electromagnetic articulography (EMA) kinematics, but these methods are limited by the scarcity of high-quality articulatory datasets. We introduce synthesizing text to articulatory kinematics (STArK), which offers a novel approach in generating articulatory kinematics directly from text. We train STArK on the LibriTTS-R dataset with pseudo-labeled EMA features derived from a pre-trained speech-to-articulation model. STArK is the first model to demonstrate high-quality articulatory kinematics generation from text, achieves comparable performance to existing multi-speaker text-to-speech models at speech synthesis, and supports voice cloning without seeing speaker embeddings during training."
