---
title: "Speaker Diarization in Static and Egocentric Videos via Speech-Face Alignment"
authors: "Zeyang Zhang, <strong>Xavier Yin</strong>, Zhaobo Zheng, Kumar Akash, Teruhisa Misu, Carlos Busso"
venue: "ACM International Conference on Multimodal Interaction (ICMI '26), Napoli, Italy"
date: 2026-10-05
abstract: "The ubiquity of personal devices, including smart glasses, opens up opportunities for multimodal processing in highly heterogeneous and dynamic settings. We investigate multimodal solutions that effectively leverage multiple microphones and cameras to support collaborative human tasks. A fundamental task in this context is speaker diarization, which determines who is speaking at any given time. We introduce static-egocentric audio-visual diarization (SEAD), a novel approach for speaker diarization that achieves multi-channel audio diarization by fusing audio information with facial information extracted from static exo- and dynamic egocentric video recordings. SEAD incorporates face tracking, cross-video and cross-audio person matching, and audio-visual fusion, and uses a fine-tuned audio-visual active speaker detector that recovers missed speech, suppresses false alarms, and reduces speaker confusion. Evaluated on a novel multi-person, multi-perspective group collaboration dataset, SEAD improves diarization performance compared to an audio-only baseline, reducing diarization error rate (DER) from 28.65% to 15.83% and the Jaccard error rate (JER) from 29.84% to 17.05%. Our findings demonstrate the potential of combining static and dynamic video perspectives to strengthen multimodal diarization in collaborative scenarios."
